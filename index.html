Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Routine Advanced

Introduction: The Complete Blood Count is a foundational hematological assay used to evaluate overall health and detect disorders. It provides quantitative data on red cells, white cells, and platelets.

Aim

To measure the cellular components of blood, aiding in diagnosis of anemia, infection, and hematological malignancies.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify normal ranges for RBC, WBC, Hb, Hct, and platelets.
  • Interpret CBC results in clinical context.
  • Recognize common errors and troubleshoot.

Scientific Principles

Impedance counting, light scatter, and fluorescence flow cytometry. Automated analyzers use electrical resistance (Coulter principle) to count and size cells.


Blood smear microscopic view (illustrative)

Theory

Blood is composed of formed elements (RBC, WBC, platelets) suspended in plasma. CBC quantifies these elements, providing indices like MCV, MCH, and MCHC.

Apparatus & Materials

  • Automated hematology analyzer (e.g., Sysmex XN).
  • EDTA vacutainer tubes.
  • Microscope and slides.
  • RBC/WBC diluting fluids.

Chemicals/Reagents

  • Isoton
  • Lysing reagent
  • Stain (Wright-Giemsa)

Experimental Setup


Flowchart: Sample collection → Dilution → Counting → Data processing

Procedure

  1. Collect venous blood into EDTA tube.
  2. Mix gently, load onto analyzer.
  3. Run automated cycle: RBC, WBC, Hb, Hct, PLT.
  4. Review histogram and flags.

Observations

Typical histogram: RBC peak at ~90 fL, WBC differential, platelet cluster.

Calculations

MCV = (Hct / RBC) × 10 (fL); MCH = (Hb / RBC) × 10 (pg).

Interpretation

Low Hb suggests anemia; elevated WBC indicates infection or leukemia; thrombocytopenia may be due to bone marrow failure.

Common Errors

  • Clotted sample → falsely low counts.
  • Hemolysis → elevated potassium, interfered Hb.

Troubleshooting

If flags appear, prepare a blood smear and perform manual differential.

Applications

Pre-surgical evaluation, monitoring chemotherapy, infectious disease workup.

Advantages

Rapid, reproducible, provides multiple parameters.

Limitations

Does not assess bone marrow morphology; may require reflex testing.

Safety Precautions

  • Use gloves and PPE.
  • Dispose of sharps in puncture-proof container.

Waste Disposal

Autoclave or incinerate biological waste.

Clinical Importance

Essential for diagnosing anemia, polycythemia, leukemias, and thrombocytopenias.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a "shift to the left"? A: Increased immature neutrophils, seen in bacterial infection.
Q: Can I run CBC on capillary blood? A: Yes, but results may vary; venous is preferred.

Quick Summary

CBC is a rapid, routine test that provides essential blood cell counts. Interpret with clinical context.
